About

          

        


        

          

            

              My journey into data engineering and machine learning didn’t start with pristine datasets—it began on the factory floor and in the clinical back-office.
            

            

              With a foundational background in Mechatronics Engineering, my early career was spent extracting signal from physical noise. I built industrial computer vision systems and managed complex sensor kinematics to automate manufacturing workflows. Today, I apply that same rigorous, systems-level thinking to the digital world: architecting the scalable data pipelines and machine learning infrastructure that power intelligent, real-world applications.
            

            

              I don't just train models; I build the ecosystems that sustain them.

              Whether I am orchestrating containerized ETL pipelines with Apache Airflow, designing dimensional data warehouses in PostgreSQL, or deploying predictive time-series models for asset valuation, I treat data engineering as the foundational lifeblood of Applied AI. I specialize in bridging the gap between raw, multi-source data and production-ready MLOps architectures.
